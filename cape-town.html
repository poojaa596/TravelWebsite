Discover Cape Town

Cape Town is a port city on South Africa's southwest coast, on a peninsula beneath the imposing Table Mountain. Slowly rotating cable cars climb to the mountain's flat top, from which there are sweeping views of the city, the busy harbor and boats heading for Robben Island, the notorious prison that once held Nelson Mandela, which is now a living museum.

The city is known for its harbor, for its natural setting in the Cape Floristic Region, and for landmarks such as Table Mountain and Cape Point. As the oldest urban area in South Africa, Cape Town was developed by the Dutch East India Company (VOC) as a supply station for Dutch ships sailing to East Africa, India, and the Far East. Today it is one of the most multicultural cities in the world, reflecting its role as a major destination for immigrants and expatriates.

Suggested 7-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival & V&A Waterfront

Arrive in Cape Town, check into hotel. Evening exploring Victoria & Alfred Waterfront.

Day 2: Table Mountain & City Tour

Morning cable car up Table Mountain. Afternoon city tour including Bo-Kaap.

Day 3: Robben Island & Beaches

Morning ferry to Robben Island. Afternoon at Camps Bay or Clifton beaches.

Day 4: Cape Peninsula Tour

Full day tour to Cape of Good Hope, Boulders Beach penguins, and Chapman's Peak.

Day 5: Winelands Excursion

Full day in Stellenbosch and Franschhoek with wine tastings and gourmet lunch.

Day 6: Kirstenbosch & Shopping

Morning at Kirstenbosch Botanical Gardens. Afternoon shopping in city center.

Day 7: Departure

Morning at leisure before departure.
